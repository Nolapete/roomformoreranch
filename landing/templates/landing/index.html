About Myotonic Goats

Myotonic goats, also known as fainting goats, are a unique breed known for their muscle-stiffening trait caused by a genetic condition called myotonia congenita. Despite their name, they do not lose consciousness when they "faint." These goats originated in the United States in the 1880s, particularly in Tennessee.

Why Choose Myotonic Goats?

  • Low Maintenance: Ideal for beginners
  • Gentle Temperament: Calm and friendly
  • Excellent Meat Quality: High muscle-to-fat ratio
  • Unique and Harmless Trait

Care & Feeding

These goats do well on pasture with fresh water, shelter, and supplemental feed. Like all goats, they need regular hoof trimming and vaccinations to stay healthy.

Fun Facts

  • Fainting varies in frequency from goat to goat
  • They come in a variety of colors and sizes
  • Some people keep them solely as pets
